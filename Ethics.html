Core Mandate

Ethics of Invisibility.

EchoLink was founded on a singular premise: the data we gather should never cost the subject its well-being. We have engineered out the "Labor Wall" and the "Handling Stress" typical of traditional VHF telemetry.

The 3% Mass Threshold

Standard guidelines allow for 5% body-weight tags. EchoLink targets a <3% threshold (approx. 0.25g) to ensure zero impact on flight kinematics, foraging efficiency, or metabolic burn during critical pre-hibernation periods.

Biocompatible Housing

Utilizing a proprietary blend of 3D-printed chitin (bat-native material) and hemp fiber. Should a node be groomed off or dropped, it leaves zero microplastic footprint in the karst environment.

Zero-Capture Recurrence

By utilizing NTN (Non-Terrestrial Network) satellite uplinks, we eliminate the need for researchers to re-enter caves or re-mist-net subjects to download data. One touch, one season of data.

Autonomous Detachment

Our surgical-grade adhesive is calibrated to degrade over a 4-month window. The node naturally detaches before the spring emergence, ensuring no long-term interference with the subject's lifecycle.

"The observer effect should be zero."

Our ethics protocol is developed in alignment with NY-DEC handling standards and USFWS Section 10(a)(1)(A) permit guidelines for endangered Myotis species.
